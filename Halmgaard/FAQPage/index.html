üö´ Automation Policy

What is automation, and why is it prohibited?

Automation refers to the use of scripts, macros, bots, or third-party tools that perform actions without direct, real-time player input.

This includes (but is not limited to):

  • Automated spell usage
  • Training dummy automation
  • Combat or skill routines
  • Any system that reduces or removes active player control

Automation is prohibited because it creates an unfair advantage and undermines the integrity of the game environment.

Are warnings issued before a ban?

üî¥ No warnings are issued.
Any verified use of automation results in an immediate and permanent ban.

This policy is strictly enforced and applies equally to all accounts.

What tools or behaviors are considered unsafe?

Any software or hardware that meets one or more of the following criteria is considered unsafe:

  • Automatically repeats inputs
  • Reacts to in-game events without player input
  • Performs actions while the player is not actively controlling the character

‚ö†Ô∏è If a tool performs actions on your behalf, it is not allowed.

Does this include training dummies?

Yes. Automation rules apply for the entire world of Halmgaard, including the Training Dummy area.

There are no exceptions based on location, activity type, or perceived impact.

‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement & Reporting

How can I report suspected automation?

Reports should be submitted through the official Discord reporting channel and should include:

  • Character name(s)
  • Date and time of the incident
  • A clear description of the observed behavior
  • Supporting evidence, if available

Incomplete or vague reports may not be actionable.

Can banned accounts be appealed?

Permanent bans issued for automation are final and are not eligible for appeal.

üéÆ Gameplay Basics

This section explains the basic controls and interface elements needed to navigate the world of Halmgaard and begin playing effectively.

Movement
  • PC: Use the Arrow Keys or W, A, S, D keys to move your character.
  • Mobile: Use the virtual joystick located in the bottom-left corner of the screen.

üí° Diagonal Movement: To move diagonally, click or press on your character and drag in the direction of the tile you want to move to.

Health, Mana, and Hunger
  • The Health Bar (red) and Mana Bar (blue) are located in the top-right corner of the screen.
  • Health and Mana regenerate by eating food.

üçñ Hunger: If you see a Hunger icon below your Experience bar, your character needs to eat food in order to continue regenerating Health and Mana.

Experience and Combat
  • The Experience Bar (green) is located below the Health and Mana bars and shows progress toward your next level.
  • Experience is gained by attacking and defeating creatures throughout the world.

To attack a creature:

  • PC: Left-click the creature or press the Space Bar
  • Mobile: Tap the creature or use the Auto-Attack button in the bottom-right corner of the screen
Inventory and Equipment

You can access your Inventory and Equipment at any time:

  • PC: Press the I key
  • Mobile: Tap the Backpack icon in the top-left corner of the screen

Your Inventory stores all collected items and allows you to equip weapons, armor, and other gear.

üìä Skills & Progression

How do skills and professions progress?

All professions progress equally in their primary combat skill. Each vocation specializes in a different core skill:

  • Knight ‚Äì Melee
  • Archer ‚Äì Accuracy
  • Sorcerer ‚Äì Magic
  • Shaman ‚Äì Magic

In addition to their primary skill, all characters also gain experience in Shielding.

Shielding experience rates:

  • Knights gain Shielding experience at the fastest rate
  • Archers gain Shielding experience at a moderate rate
  • Sorcerers & Shamans gain Shielding experience at the slowest rate
How skills gain experience

Skills progress through active use, rewarding players based on how they play.

Experience is earned by performing relevant actions during gameplay:

  • Melee ‚Äì Landing hits with melee weapons or spells in the Melee category
  • Accuracy ‚Äì Striking enemies with ranged weapons or spells in the Accuracy category
  • Magic ‚Äì Dealing damage with magic weapons or casting spells in the Magic category
  • Shielding ‚Äì Receiving attempted strikes from enemies
Skill leveling and progression curve

Skill advancement follows an experience curve.

  • Early skill levels progress quickly to help new players build momentum
  • Higher skill levels require increasing amounts of experience and consistency

There are currently no skill level caps. Skills can continue to improve indefinitely for players who are committed to training them.

Training Methods

There are three primary ways to train skills. Each method has a different training efficiency, which affects how much experience is gained per successful action.

ü™ì PvE Combat Training (100% Efficiency)

Engage monsters, creatures, and NPCs throughout the game world. This is the standard training method and provides full experience for all valid actions. It is ideal for players who want to progress naturally while exploring, questing, and looting.

‚öîÔ∏è PvP Combat Training (120% Efficiency)

Engage in combat with other players. PvP training provides increased experience gains, rewarding players who take on the unpredictability of human opponents. This method carries higher risk and generally requires more frequent healing.

üèãÔ∏è Training Dummies (75% Efficiency ‚Äì Gold Account Only)

Training dummies are available exclusively to Gold Account players. They allow for continuous, passive skill training and grant 75% of normal experience. While slower than active combat, this method is ideal for background or extended training sessions.

‚öîÔ∏è Player vs. Player (PvP)

üî¥ PvP Unlocks at Level 35
Player vs. Player combat becomes available once a character reaches level 35. To promote more tactical and engaging encounters, all PvP damage is reduced by 55%, preventing instant or unavoidable deaths.

PvP Combat Locks and Safe Zones

Players who attack others without provocation receive a PvP combat lock.

While under a combat lock:

  • You cannot enter safe zones
  • You remain flagged as being in PvP combat

Combat locks expire 1 minute after the last PvP interaction between involved players.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: If you leave combat and wait longer than one minute before re-engaging, the encounter is treated as a new PvP interaction. In this situation, you may receive a skull even if the other player is still flagged. Safe zones should be used carefully!
